02 — What I believe

Principles that guide my work.

A short list of convictions I keep coming back to—the ones that actually shape how I decide what to build and how to build it.

01.

Simplicity beats complexity.

The best software feels effortless to use. Clarity is a design decision, not an accident.

02.

Learn by building.

Reading matters, but real understanding only arrives when you've made the thing yourself—and broken it.

03.

Solve problems, don't chase trends.

Technology is only valuable when it solves a real need. Novelty for its own sake ages badly.

04.

Every project teaches something.

Even failed experiments compound. The engineer I am next year is being built by the ones I ship this year.

05.

Great software is built for people.

Behind every request there's a human trying to get something done. That's the actual customer.

06.

Fundamentals outlast frameworks.

Languages and libraries come and go. Data structures, systems thinking and clear writing keep paying interest.
